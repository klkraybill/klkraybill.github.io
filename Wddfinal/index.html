Human Connection
Many researchers agree that the happiness we feel from human connection is not just an abstract state of mind, but rather an evolutionary and biological imperative.
Lonley people have much higher illness rates becaues when they're not around other humans (and thus their bacteria and such) their inflammatory systems becomes more sensitive to outisde microbes.
The vagus nerve is intimately tied to how we connect with one another. It links directly to nerves that tune our ears to human speech and coordinate eye contact. Studies have found that higher vagal tone is associated with greater altruistic behavior.
There is research that proves the more basketball team members touche eachother the higher the score.
Social anthropology theorizes that money and status decreases functions of happiness because when one does not need things from others, they do not need to connect. This circles back to overactive immune system idea.
Pain Pathways
When people feel emotional pain, the same areas of the brain get activated as when people feel physical pain: the anterior insula and the anterior cingulate cortex.
There was a study where people with emotional pain took tylenol (acetaminophen) and reported fewer hurt feelings during that period. Brains scaled at end of treatment and the acetaminophen takers had less activation in those 2 brain areas.
Symmetry
Evolutionary theorists argue that more symmetric faces are preferred because symmetry is sign of superior genetic quality and developmental stability.
Symmetry is an alert of sexual and overall health in humans.
Estrogen and testosterone are also immunosuppressors.
Numerous studies show that nonfacial body symmetry of men positively correlates with their mating success. This pattern is not seen in women.
Attractive expressions of secondary sexual traits require high hormone titers during their construction, and thus can be afforded only by immunocompetent individuals.
